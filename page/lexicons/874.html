Summary
Ample glosses of selected words in Thomas Speight's exposition of old words in his edition of Chaucer: aketon (haketone), besante (duckett), fermentacione (dawbinge), orfrayes, oundye and crispe, resalgar, begyn and bigott, citrinatione, forage, heroner, hyppe, nowell, porpherye, sendale, trepegett, wiuer, authenticke, abandone, veracle, Campaneus, hereos, florence, and unseriall; as well as emended forms
