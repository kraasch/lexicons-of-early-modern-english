The following analysis comes from J. H. Baker and J. S. Ringrose, A Catalogue of English Legal Manuscripts in Cambridge University Library (1996):

(a) version 1, beginning "Soc hoc est quod habeatis sectam de hominibus vestris in curea vestra", and ending at "Briggebote", such as Cambridge University Library MS Dd.7.6, no. 85, fol. 19v;

(b) version 2: beginning "Soc est secta de homagio", and ending at "Briggebote", such as Cambridge University Library MS Dd.9.38, no. 92, fol. 208;

(c) version 3: beginning "Hamsocn" and ending at "Burgbote", such as Cambridge University Library MS Hh.3.11, no. 69, fol. 107.
Cf. British Library Stowe MS 1030(c): "Glossary of old English words in charters, ca. 1600-50."
